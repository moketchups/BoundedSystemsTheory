## Specific Critiques Worth Addressing

### 1. "Axiom 2 is question-begging"

The reviewers argue that Axiom 2 assumes what Theorem 1 proves. This is a valid structural observation.

**Response possibility:** Axiom 2 generalizes established results (Gödel, Turing, Chaitin). If those results are accepted, Axiom 2 follows. The question is whether the generalization is justified, not whether it's circular.

### 2. "Undefined key terms"

"Sufficiently expressive" and "self-referential" lack formal definitions.

**Response possibility:** These terms could be formally defined via reference to arithmetic expressiveness (Gödel) or universal computation (Turing). The specification could be strengthened.

### 3. "Infinite regress not ruled out"

Theorem 2 dismisses infinite regress without proof.

**Response possibility:** The specification should explicitly address why infinite regress is untenable (leads to no actual grounding) or acknowledge it as an alternative with different implications.

### 4. "LLM empirical method is flawed"

All models are transformers, trained on similar data, prone to similar outputs.

**Response possibility:** This is valid. The empirical section should be marked as illustrative rather than rigorous validation. True falsification requires diverse system types.
